Does Landscaping Increase Property Value on the South Shore?

Well-maintained landscaping can make a home feel cared for before a buyer ever steps inside. On the South Shore, where homes range from coastal properties to suburban neighborhoods and wooded lots, curb appeal can play an important role in first impressions.

High-Impact Improvements

  • Clean lawn and edges: A tidy lawn gives the property an immediate sense of care.
  • Fresh mulch beds: Mulch can make planting areas look clean and finished while helping with moisture control.
  • Walkways and patios: Hardscaping can improve outdoor living space and everyday usability.
  • Drainage improvements: Solving standing water or erosion can protect the property and improve function.
  • Low-maintenance plantings: Native or hardy plants can look attractive without creating excessive upkeep.

Why It Matters Locally

Homes in towns like Plymouth, Duxbury, Marshfield, Scituate, and Kingston may benefit from landscaping that handles coastal conditions. Inland homes in Bridgewater, Easton, Taunton, and Middleborough may benefit from larger-scale lawn care, cleanup, and wooded-lot maintenance.

Focus on Practical Improvements First

Before investing in expensive upgrades, address the basics: cleanup, edging, mulch, pruning, lawn repair, and drainage. These improvements are often easier for buyers to notice and appreciate.
